What the words mean
Treble clef
The upper staff — usually your right hand, higher notes.
Bass clef
The lower staff — usually your left hand, lower notes.
R.H. / L.H.
Right hand / left hand.
Root
The note a chord is named after (the C in a C chord).
Triad
A three-note chord: root, the note two letters up (3rd), and two more up (5th).
Major / minor
Major sounds bright; minor sounds darker. The middle note sits one key lower in minor.
I–IV–V
The 1st, 4th and 5th chords of a key. In C that’s C, F, G — the backbone of most songs.
Inversion
The same chord with its notes re-stacked, so the lowest note changes.
Flat-7
Adds a note that gives a chord its bluesy, “wants-to-resolve” sound (e.g. B♭ in C7).
Finger numbers
The small digits by a note: 1 = thumb, 2 = index, 3 = middle, 4 = ring, 5 = little finger. R.H. above the note, L.H. below.
BPM
Beats per minute — the metronome’s speed. Start slow, speed up only when clean.
